Sunda Pangolin

Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Mammalia
Order: Pholidota
Family: Manidae
Genus: Manis
Species: M. javanica
Habitat: Forests and Shrublands
Diet: Insectivore
Average Life Span: Unknown
Size: Up to 4 feet (in length)
Weight: Up to 11 pounds


Introduction

Sunda Pangolins, which also can be known as Malayan Pangolins, are approximately 1.2 m long. These pangolins differ from other pangolins by having lesser rows of scales, longer tails and shorter forelimb claws. Compared to other species of pangolins, Sunda Pangolins are the most widely distributed pangolins, which can be found in several countries, including Malaysia, Myanmar, Thailand and Vietnam. Sunda Pangolins feed by using their long, sticky tongue and their main sources of food are ants and termites.

Currently, the average lifespan of Sunda Pangolins is unknown as the pangolins are difficult to monitor and study. With a gestation period of around three to four months, female pangolins most commonly give birth to only one offspring. At birth, the scales are pink in colour and soft.

Why are they facing extinction

Illegal Wildlife Trade

Sunda Pangolins are often hunted to trade their body parts for a high price. In countries, such as Vietnam and China, pangolin meat is viewed as a luxurious product. Furthermore, the scales of these pangolins are used in traditional medicines for different purposes, including improving blood circulation as well as curing skin aliment.
