CHINESE EGRETS

Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Aves
Order: Pelecaniformes
Family: Ardeidae
Genus: Egretta
Species: E. eulophotes
Habitat: Shallow tidal estuaries and mudflats
Diet: Small fishes,shrimps and crabs


Introduction

Chinese Egrets are typically around 68cm in height. They are identified from their white plumage eventhough they have slight resemblance with the Little Egret Egretta Garzetta.

These Chinese Egrets have a dusky bill with it`s basal portion being in flesh coloured and their legs in yellow-green during the off-breeding season. Whereas during the active breeding season, the Chinese Egrets adults will start to build a luxuriant crest which spans 11cm long and a long lanceolate plumes on it`s breast.

Egrets are known for it`s agility in looking and catching it`s prey for food. They will usually feed in open tidal lagoons or nest where they are able to easily spot it`s prey. For these species of Egrets, they do have a favourite technique which they usually enjoys is running back and fro while having it`s wings half-spread, flappped or flicked and repeating stabbing in the water.

Chinese Egrets are commonly found along the west and east coast of Kota Kinabalu and Sandakan Bay.



Why is it facing extinction?

Facing major extinction threat due to demands on the egrets`s plumes and eggs as it is believed that after consuming, it will improves one skin. Other than that, the nuptial plumes of Chinese Egrets were in demand for decorating hats, has been one of the contributions on the declination of the species count.
The reclamation of the tidal flats and estuarine habitats for infrastructural building has been causing the Chinese Egrets to lose it`s natural habitat.
It has been classified as Vulnerable in the IUCN Red List as the last recorded count for this Chinese Egret is 100.
