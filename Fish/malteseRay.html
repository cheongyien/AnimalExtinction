Maltese Ray

Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Chondrichthyes
Order: Rajiformes
Family: Rajidae
Genus: Leucoraja
Species: L. melitensis
Habitat: Open seas
Geographic Range: Mediterranean Sea off the coasts of Algeria, Italy, Malta and Tunisia
Diet: Carnivores
Reproduction: Oviparous
Other Name: Maltese Skate


Introduction

Maltese Skate lives in Marine Neritic or Marine Deep Benthic. They occur most commonly at 400-800m. The last encounter of Maltese Skate was during bottom trawl surveys in 2005. They feed on crustceans, mainly amphipods. They practice distinct pairing with embrace and breeds throughout the year but ripe females are observed mainly in spring and autumn. Young may tend to follow large objects, such as their mother. They are oviparous animals that produce young by means of egg. Embryos feed solely on yolk and eventually the egg will hatch.

Why are they facing extinction

The habitat where Maltese Skate lives is where trawling activity is intensive. It was usually taken as bycatch that often end up being discarded and only the large ones are for human consumption. However, Maltest Skate is now extremely rare along Tunisan coast. They are neither exploited nor traded commercially. Their habitat loss is mainly their cause of extinction.
