European Eel

Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Actinopterygii
Order: Anguilliformes
Family: Anguillidae
Genus: Anguilla

Genus: Anguilla

Geographic Range: English Channel, Coasts of the Mediterranean Sea, Northern Atlantic Ocean from Iceland to Mauritania, Baltic and North Seas
Habitat: marine, freshwater, brackish aquatic environments (depending of lifestage)
Diet: carnivore (insectivore, molluscivore, scavenger)
Sexual Dimorphism: female larger
Common Name: Silver Eel, Yellow Eel
Life Span: Up to 80 years


Introduction

European Eel is a solitary species. It migrates by swimming with ocean currents to various regions during different stages of their life. They are usually active during the day. and are found most often on the floor of the ocean or river or at depths of 0-700m.European Eel migrate to the Sargasso sea during late winter to early spring for breeding. They breed only once during their lifetime and die after spawning. They using their sense of taste to sense the environment. Juvenile European Eel aka Glass Eel prey on insect larvae, dead fish and small crustaceans. However, adults have broad diet where they prey on aquatic invertebrates and will eat up anything they could find. They also eat freshwater,marine or terrestrial fauna.

Why are they facing extinction

Overfishing, reduction of water quality, parasites such as Anguillicola crassus, barriers to migration, and natural changes. Hydroelectric dams has blocked or prevent European Eel to reach Sargasso sea for spawning and migration. The Gulf Stream, North Atlantic oscillation and North Atlantic drift are natural changes that have prevent migration of European Eel Land reclaimation and construction of dam have caused habitat degradation and water quality reduction that make European Eel hard to survive.
